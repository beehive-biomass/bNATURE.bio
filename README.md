# bNATURE.bio

Hemp biomass lots: what is being sold, and who says so.

A grade is an **attestation**, never a fact. Every value carries who measured it, by what
method, against whose standard, and when — and the type makes it impossible to show a grade
without its attestor.

The attestation machinery itself lives in
[`attestation-core`](https://github.com/beehive-nature/attestation-core), which is neutral,
permissively licensed, and belongs to no consumer. **This tree holds only what is actually
hemp:** the § 297A statutory material categories, the analytes, the FSA seed-label facts,
and the total-THC arithmetic.

## The type that keeps a container from shipping

A Mexican certificate of analysis measuring **delta-9 THC only**, evaluated against the US
**total-THC** standard — delta-9 plus 0.877 × THCA — never measured the decarboxylated
fraction. The honest answer is `NotDetermined`, naming the missing analyte. **Never
*fails*.**

A shipment refused on a document that proves nothing is a different injustice from one
refused on a document that proves something, and the type refuses to blur them.

## What this deliberately does not contain

No price, no quality score, no rating, no rank, no token. A lot is a description of matter,
not an offer and not an economy. Aggregating attestations into one number destroys the
provenance the model exists to preserve — sort by an attribute if you like; this type will
not decide what "better" means.

`Attestor` has no `Verified` variant and no `Certified` boolean. `SelfReported` is honest
and useful, and most of agriculture runs on it — what must never happen is a self-reported
figure rendering identically to a laboratory result.

Each absence has a test. Removing one deletes the explanation for why it must not exist.

**Nothing here is legal advice.**
